In 2021, I received the call no person ever wants to receive. My gynechologist called to tell me that my recent diagnostic tests came back showing that I had Invasive Lobular Carcinoma. BREAST CANCER. He proceeded to tell me that it's a more rare form, only 15% of BC cases are typically ILC. Needless to say, I was in shock. What led to this diagnosis was a series of tests which started with an annual mammogram. I had been getting mammograms since I was 35 because my mother was diagnosed with Breast Cancer that year. At 41, this annual test had become a mere formality. I never thought I'd get cancer!

I'm here to tell you that while a Breast Cancer diagnosis is scary, arming yourself with knowledge will help in the fight and calm your fears!

"Cancer sure has a way of helping you put life in perspective" - Janelle Antoine

Coping with a Diagnosis

People cope with the news of a diagnosis in different ways. In my case, to help with fear and anxiety I talked to people in my support system regularly about what I was feeling. They gave me permission to have whatever emotion surfaced.

Also, the weekend prior to my surgery to remove the cancer, I got a new puppy! A rambunctious 8-week old Vizsla. Pets make everything better.
